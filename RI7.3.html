Core Standards

RI.7.3 Analyze interactions between individuals, events, and ideas.

RL.7.1 Cite evidence for explicit & inferred claims.

L.7.4 Determine meaning via context clues & word parts.

Essential Question

How do specific ideas and individuals interact to shape the events of the Salem Witch Trials?

Learning Activity 1: Direct Instruction & Modeling

Activating Strategy: "The Gossip Simulation" via digital sticky notes on Canvas.

Secret: "Starting next Monday, the school is replacing all cafeteria chairs with giant exercise balls..."

The Interaction Triangle (Graphic Organizer):
  • Top Point: The Idea (Fear of the unknown).
  • Bottom Left: The Individual (Abigail Williams).
  • Bottom Right: The Event (The first accusation).

Assessment Prompt: Infer why the community believed "spectral evidence" without physical proof.

Learning Activity 2: The Ripple Effect

RI.7.3 Logic Strand: Individual/Idea + Action = Change in Event.

Assignment: Students work in pairs on Google Slides to analyze a specific section of the text.

The Ripple Effect Map Logic:
  • Inner Circle: The Idea (Fear of the Devil).
  • Middle Circle: The Individuals (Abigail Williams, Tituba).
  • Outer Circle: The Event (The Trials and Executions).

Summarizing Strategy: "If the Idea of 'witchcraft' were replaced with 'illness,' would the Events have changed? Explain."

Day 3 & 4: Deep Analysis & Comparison

Day 3: The "Trial of Ideas"

Collaborative practice on Google Slides ("The Courtroom Flip"). Students find 3 quotes where assigned individuals (Abigail, Tituba, or Judge) interacted with fear or religion to cause an event.

Day 4: Comparative Analysis

Activity: "Hand Up, Stand Up, Pair Up" using vocabulary cards (e.g., Scapegoat). Students then research a modern "Viral Rumor" or "Social Media Trend" to compare to 1692 Salem.

Summarizing Strategy (3-Sentence Summary):
  1. State the main individual.
  2. State the main event.
  3. Explain the idea that connected them.

Differentiation & Scaffolding

Support (Scaffolding): Interaction word bank: incited, feared, caused, influenced.
Challenge (Extension): Independent research on modern events fueled by specific underlying ideas.
