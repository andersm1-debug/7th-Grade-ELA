Learning-Focused Lesson Plan

Instructor: M. Anderson | Topic: RI7.3 / RL7.1

Standards & Goals

Core Standards

RI.7.3 Analyze interactions between individuals, events, and ideas.

RL.7.1 Cite textual evidence to support analysis and inferences.

L.7.4 Determine meaning of unknown/multiple-meaning words.

Essential Question

How do specific ideas and individuals interact to shape the events of a text?

Daily Routine

Activity Time Allocation
Daily Free Writes7 Minutes
Daily Grammar Practice (DGP)5-10 Minutes
Mugshots3 Minutes
Weekly Vocabulary5-20 Minutes

Instructional Path

Activating Strategy: "The Gossip Simulation"

Similiar to the "Telephone game". Students receive a "secret" and must quickly pass it around the room. We observe how the idea changes as it is passed along, influencing student actions.

Learning Activity 1: Direct Instruction

Text: "Understanding the Salem Witch Trials" (PDF via Google Drive)

Modeling: Interactive reading using digital pens on the SmartBoard / TV to categorize Individuals, Events, and Ideas.

Graphic Organizer: The Interaction Triangle (Idea, Individual, Event).Each line connecting the points of a triangle must "connect" using complete sentences.

Learning Activity 2 & 3: The Ripple Effect

Students use Google Slides to map causal relationships. Smaller inner circle = Idea, Slightly bigger circle = Indiviuals, Larger circle = Events. Prompt: "If the Idea of 'witchcraft' were replaced with 'illness,' would the Events have changed?"

Day 4: The "Modern Hysteria" Connection

Comparison between 1692 Salem and modern-day Social Media trends/rumors.

Differentiation & Extensions

  • Support: Provided "Word Bank" for graphic organizers (incited, feared, influenced).
  • Extension: Independent research into modern viral rumors and the "ideas" fueling them.
