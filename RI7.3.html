Standards

RI.7.3 Analyze interactions between individuals, events, and ideas.

RL.7.1 Cite evidence for explicit and inferred claims.

L.7.4 Determine meaning via context clues & reference materials.

Learning Goals

Students Will Know:

  • Role of Context: How fear (idea) can lead individuals to act in ways they normally wouldn't.
  • Vocabulary: Definitions and usage (e.g., Infer, Scapegoat).
  • Essential Question: How an author uses literary elements to develop central theme.

Students Will Be Able To:

  • Cite Evidence (RL.7.1) from the teleplay.
  • Map causal relationships (RI.7.3).
  • Determine meaning via context clues (L.7.4).
  • Apply Grammar (Mugshots/DGP).

Lesson Essential Question: How do specific ideas and individuals interact to shape the events of the Salem Witch Trials?

Learning Activity 1: Direct Instruction & Modeling

Activating Strategy: "The Gossip Simulation" (Canvas Digital Sticky Notes). Secret: "Starting next Monday, the school is replacing all cafeteria chairs with giant exercise balls..."

The Interaction Triangle:
  • Top Point: The Idea (Fear of the unknown).
  • Bottom Left: The Individual (Abigail Williams).
  • Bottom Right: The Event (The first accusation).

Summarizing Strategy: Based on the text, infer why the community believed "spectral evidence" without physical proof.

Learning Activity 2: The Ripple Effect

RI.7.3 Logic Strand: Individual/Idea + Action = Change in Event.

Assignment: Pair analysis on Google Slides to identify one specific "Idea" and three resulting "Events."

The Ripple Effect Map Logic:
  • Inner Circle: The Idea (Fear of the Devil).
  • Middle Circle: The Individuals (Abigail Williams, Tituba).
  • Outer Circle: The Event (The Trials and Executions).

Assessment Prompt: "If the Idea of 'witchcraft' or 'Spectral Evidence' were replaced with 'illness,' would the Events have changed?"

Day 3 & 4: Analysis & Comparison

Day 3: The "Trial of Ideas"

Courtroom Flip: Groups assigned roles (Abigail, Tituba, or Judge) to find 3 quotes where they interacted with an Idea (fear, religion, gossip) to cause an Event.

Day 4: Modern Hysteria & Vocabulary

Activating: Hand Up, Stand Up, Pair Up with vocab cards. Context Clue Detective: Define "Scapegoat" from text before checking Canvas glossary.

Comparison: Research a "Viral Rumor" or "Social Media Trend" using the Ripple Effect Map.

3-Sentence Summary: 1. Main Individual | 2. Main Event | 3. Connecting Idea.

Scaffolding & Extensions

Support: Interaction word bank (incited, feared, caused, influenced).
Extension: Research a modern "Event" and identify the fueling "Idea."
