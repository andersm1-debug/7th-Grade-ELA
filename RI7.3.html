Standards

RI.7.3 Analyze interactions between individuals, events, and ideas.

RL.7.1 Cite several pieces of textual evidence to support analysis.

L.7.4 Determine meaning of unknown/multiple-meaning words.

Learning Goals

Students Will Know:

  • Role of Context: How fear influences behavior.
  • The Essential Question: How authors use literary elements to develop theme.
  • Definitions of: Infer, Scapegoat, Explicit, Cite.

Students Will Be Able To:

  • Map causal relationships between ideas and events.
  • Apply "Interactive Reading" with digital pens/SmartBoard.
  • Compare 1692 Salem interactions to modern Social Media trends.

The RI.7.3 Logic Strand

The Ripple Effect Formula: Individual/Idea + Action = Change in Event.

Essential Question:

How do specific ideas and individuals interact to shape the events of the Salem Witch Trials?

Learning Activities

1. Direct Instruction & Modeling

Activating Strategy: "Gossip" Simulation via Canvas digital sticky notes.

The Interaction Triangle: Modeling how Ideas (Fear) influence Individuals (Abigail) to create Events (The Accusations).

2. Deep Analysis: "The Trial of Ideas"

The Courtroom Flip: Collaborative Google Slides activity. Groups find quotes where characters (Tituba, Judge, etc.) interacted with fear or religion to cause specific legal events.

3. Comparative Analysis

Modern Connection: Students research a viral rumor/trend and use a Ripple Effect Map to compare modern hysteria to 1692 Salem.

Summarizing Strategy: 3-Sentence Summary (Individual -> Event -> Connecting Idea).

Scaffolding & Extension

Support: Provided Word Bank for Interaction Triangle (e.g., incited, feared, caused).
Extension: Independent research on modern "Events" fueled by specific underlying "Ideas."
